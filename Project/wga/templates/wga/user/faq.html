Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is this research study?

You are helping us study how humans approach analogical reasoning: What kinds of arguments do people make? What similarities do people find important?


How do I log in?

To participate in this research study, you must have already submitted the HIT titled Study on Critical Thinking - P0 Scheduling. There, you were asked to enter an anonymous username. You will be using that same exact username when you log in.


I do not have the log in key. How can I get my key?

Please go to mTurk and look for the HIT titled Study on Critical Thinking. If you already completed the HIT titled Study on Critical Thinking - P0 Scheduling, then you were given a qualification to do Study on Critical Thinking. You will find the log in key in that HIT.


My credentials do not seem to work. Am I doing something wrong?

Please review this list:

  • Are you scheduled for the correct date & time?
  • The username is case-sensitive; please make sure you have the correct capitalization
  • If you still cannot log in, email Dr. John Licato (amhr.usf@gmail.com)

My opponent is unresponsive. What do I do?

We will be actively monitoring your tasks. If you have an unresponsive opponent, please be patient with us as we partner you with new, responsive opponents. Understand that an unresponsive opponent will not affect your payment nor the time you spend on the tasks; you will spend no more than 90 minutes.
